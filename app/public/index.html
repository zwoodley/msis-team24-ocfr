Who we are...

Oconee County Fire Rescue has continued to serve the surrounding community since its inception in 1875. Over the last 150+ years OCFR has remained a fully independent, volunteer ran fire department holding an ISO (Insurance Services Organization) rating of 1; the highest given. This is an impressive feat given our vast territory covering 186 square miles holding a population of approximately 40,000 citizens.

Currently, our services are provided by 20+ volunteers, many of which hold certifications from leading institutions such as Indiana Universities Kelley School of Firefighting Science. Our Ranks include Firefighter, Engineer, Lieutenant, Captain, and Chief. Collectively, our current volunteers make up 250+ years of experience, with many coming from DeKalb County Fire Rescue, located in the northern suburbs of Metropolitan Atlanta. Our department hopes to establish OCFR as a full-career department by the year 2025, in which we will establish a formal hiring process to ensure we are able to continue meeting the needs of our constituents. Interested in joining OCFR? Please reach out to our office at (404) 596-8634.

Our Commander in Chief...

Oconee County Fire Rescue is fortunate enough to have Dr. Pipin Brabhakar serving as our 24th Oconee County Commander in Chief. Brabhakar came to the Oconee County area from Cincinatti, OH in 2009 with one goal in mind: tax the poor and rebuild OCFR's fleet to be one of the best in Northern America. Previously, Brabhakar was the Commander in Chief for the University of Cincinatti's Fire Department, in this role he was instrumental in forming a partnership with the UC Bursar Office which held students with delinquent accounts liable for fighting fires and responding to emergencies in the surrounding territory.
